# Kominka (古民家) Specialist Sites Report

## Research Overview

This report documents specialized traditional Japanese house (minka/kominka) resources for the Akiya Japan app. Kominka refers to traditional Japanese farmhouses and townhouses, typically built before 1945 using traditional timber framing methods.

---

## 1. 古民家住まいる (Kominka Sumairu)

**URL:** https://kominka.net  
**Operator:** 一般社団法人住まい教育推進協会 (Housing Education Promotion Association)  
**Contact:** TEL: 03-6275-0796

### Property Count
- Active listings: 25+ properties (varies by month)
- Nationwide coverage across 47 prefectures
- Partnership with 全国古民家再生協会 (National Kominka Restoration Association)

### Price Ranges
| Type | Price Range | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 0 Yen Kominka | FREE | Building only - buyer pays relocation/renovation |
| Standard Sale | ¥2M - ¥25M | Varies by size, location, condition |
| Premium/Historic | ¥25M+ | Cultural properties, large estates |

### Example Listings
- **0 Yen Project (滋賀県):** 120-year-old "Ikashiki Yogo-type" kominka, 135.5㎡ building, 1,220㎡ land
- **山形県:** 150-year-old house connected to Date Masamune's retainers
- **福岡県:** 0 Yen project - mountain/river surrounded curved house
- **兵庫県:** National Registered Cultural Property "Musoan" - former samurai residence

### Unique Features
- **「古民家再生総合調査報告書」or「木造住宅簡易鑑定書」** included with properties
- Pre-purchase building inspections available
- Post-purchase support from National Kominka Restoration Association
- Registered real estate agents include **古民家鑑定士** (Kominka Appraisers)
- Specialized in preserving **伝統構法** (traditional construction methods)

### Certification Requirements
- None for buyers
- Sellers can list through partner real estate agencies
- Building condition reports available for most properties

### Relocation Costs
- Dismantling: ¥1M - ¥5M+ (depending on size)
- Transportation: ¥500K - ¥3M+ (distance dependent)
- Reconstruction: ¥5M - ¥20M+

## 5. Key Considerations for Kominka Purchase

### Typical Costs (Beyond Purchase Price)

| Item | Estimated Cost | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| Building Inspection | ¥100K - ¥300K | Recommended |
| Dismantling | ¥1M - ¥5M | For relocation |
| Transportation | ¥500K - ¥3M | Distance dependent |
| Reconstruction | ¥5M - ¥20M+ | Foundation, assembly |
| Renovation | ¥3M - ¥15M+ | Modernization |
| Seismic Retrofit | ¥1M - ¥5M | Essential for older buildings |
| Land (if purchased) | Varies widely | Often available separately |

### Traditional Construction Terms
- **大黒柱 (Daikokubashira):** Central pillar
- **梁 (Hari):** Beams
- **束 (Tsuka):** Short posts
- **鴨居 (Kamoi):** Door/window frames
- **障子 (Shoji):** Paper screens
- **欄間 (Ranma):** Transom panels
- **蔵 (Kura):** Storehouse
- **土間 (Doma):** Earthen floor area
- **茅葺 (Kayabuki):** Thatched roof
- **瓦葺 (Kawarabuki):** Tile roof

### Inspection Checklist
- [ ] Structural integrity of main pillars
- [ ] Roof condition (leaks, sagging)
- [ ] Foundation condition
- [ ] Termite damage
- [ ] Moisture/dry rot
- [ ] Traditional joinery condition
- [ ] Reusability of traditional materials
- [ ] Seismic resistance assessment

## Research Notes

- Most kominka sites are Japanese-language only
- Foreign buyers may need Japanese assistance
- Building inspections are highly recommended
- Relocation costs often exceed building value
- Many municipalities offer subsidies for renovation
- Traditional craftsmen (大工/carpenters) specializing in kominka are valuable resources
